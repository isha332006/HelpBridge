Overview

India faces numerous challenges in disaster management due to its unique geo-climatic conditions and socio-economic vulnerabilities. These include floods, droughts, cyclones, earthquakes, and man-made disasters such as industrial accidents and terrorism.

Key National Issues

  • Lack of Preparedness: Insufficient early warning systems and preparedness measures in vulnerable areas.
  • Urbanization: Rapid urban growth without adequate infrastructure increases disaster risks.
  • Climate Change: Rising temperatures and unpredictable weather patterns exacerbate natural disasters.
  • Poverty: Limited resources make it harder for affected communities to recover.
  • Coordination Challenges: Inefficient coordination between central, state, and local authorities during emergencies.

Government Initiatives

The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has implemented policies like the National Disaster Management Plan (NDMP) to address these issues. These initiatives aim to build resilience through community participation, capacity building, and technology-driven strategies.
